## OSINT Sources for Monitoring Critical Minerals

### Trade Data & Supply Chains

* [UN Comtrade](https://comtrade.un.org/)
  Global trade database for tracking imports/exports of critical minerals.

* [International Trade Centre – Trade Map](https://www.trademap.org/)
  Useful for analyzing bilateral trade flows and dependencies.

* [Panjiva](https://panjiva.com/)
  Supply chain intelligence and shipment tracking.

* [ImportGenius](https://www.importgenius.com/)
  Customs and shipment data for tracking suppliers and buyers.

---

### Geological & Production Data

* [US Geological Survey (USGS)](https://www.usgs.gov/)
  Reports on mineral production, reserves, and global supply.

* [British Geological Survey (BGS)](https://www.bgs.ac.uk/)
  Data on global mineral resources and supply risks.

---

### Market Intelligence

* [Benchmark Mineral Intelligence](https://www.benchmarkminerals.com/)
  Market insights on critical minerals and tech supply chains.

* [S&P Global Commodity Insights](https://www.spglobal.com/commodityinsights/)
  Analysis of commodity markets and supply trends.

---

### Government & Policy Sources

* [European Commission – Critical Raw Materials](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/raw-materials_en)
  Policy updates and strategic dependency assessments.

* [U.S. Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS)](https://www.bis.doc.gov/)
  Export controls and strategic materials monitoring.

## OSINT Indicators to Track

When monitoring critical minerals, key signals include:

* export restrictions or bans
* new mining or refining projects
* geopolitical tensions affecting supply
* price spikes or volatility
