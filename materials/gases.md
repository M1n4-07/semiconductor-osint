# Industrial Gases in Semiconductor Manufacturing

Industrial gases are critical inputs in semiconductor manufacturing, particularly in lithography, etching, and deposition processes.

These gases are highly specialized and often depend on a limited number of suppliers, making them a key vulnerability in the semiconductor supply chain.

---

## Key Gases

### Neon

**Use:**

* excimer lasers in lithography (DUV/EUV)
* essential for chip manufacturing

**Key Producers:**

* Ukraine (historically major supplier)
* China

**Risks:**

* supply disruptions due to conflict
* dependence on a small number of producers

**Case Study:**
The war in Ukraine disrupted global neon supply, impacting semiconductor production worldwide.

---

### Helium

**Use:**

* cooling of semiconductor equipment
* controlled manufacturing environments

**Key Producers:**

* United States
* Qatar
* Algeria

**Risks:**

* limited global reserves
* logistical constraints
* price volatility

---

### Argon

**Use:**

* wafer processing
* inert atmosphere in manufacturing

**Key Producers:**

* global industrial gas companies

**Risks:**

* dependency on industrial gas infrastructure

---

### Hydrogen

**Use:**

* deposition processes
* chemical reactions in chip fabrication

**Key Producers:**

* global

**Risks:**

* energy dependency
* transport and storage challenges

---

## Key Companies

* Air Liquide
* Linde
* Air Products

These companies dominate the supply of industrial gases used in semiconductor manufacturing.

---

## OSINT Indicators to Monitor

Key signals to track:

* disruptions in production (conflicts, industrial incidents)
* export restrictions
* price increases
* supply agreements between companies and fabs
* infrastructure developments (gas plants, logistics hubs)

---

## Strategic Relevance

Industrial gases are a critical bottleneck in semiconductor manufacturing.

Disruptions in supply can:

* halt production in fabs
* increase costs
* create cascading effects across the global tech supply chain
