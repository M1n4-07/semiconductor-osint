# Critical Minerals in Semiconductor Manufacturing

Semiconductor production depends on a set of highly specialized and strategically sensitive raw materials.

These materials are often geographically concentrated, making them critical from a supply chain and geopolitical risk perspective.

---

## Key Minerals

### Gallium

**Use:**

* compound semiconductors (GaAs, GaN)
* power electronics
* 5G infrastructure
* defense systems

**Key Producers:**

* China (~90% of global supply)

**Risks:**

* export restrictions
* supply concentration
* geopolitical leverage

---

### Germanium

**Use:**

* fiber optics
* infrared optics
* high-performance electronics
* military applications

**Key Producers:**

* China
* Russia
* Canada

**Risks:**

* dual-use sensitivity
* export controls
* limited substitutes

---

### Silicon (Polysilicon)

**Use:**

* semiconductor wafers
* integrated circuits

**Key Producers:**

* China
* Germany
* United States
* Japan

**Risks:**

* energy-intensive production
* supply chain dependencies
* industrial policy exposure

---

### Cobalt

**Use:**

* semiconductor interconnects
* advanced electronics
* batteries (indirect relevance)

**Key Producers:**

* Democratic Republic of Congo

**Risks:**

* geopolitical instability
* ESG concerns
* supply concentration

---

### Tungsten

**Use:**

* chip interconnects
* high-temperature applications

**Key Producers:**

* China

**Risks:**

* supply dominance
* strategic dependency

---

### Copper

**Use:**

* wiring and interconnects
* semiconductor packaging

**Key Producers:**

* Chile
* Peru
* China

**Risks:**

* demand pressure
* infrastructure dependency

---

## Analytical Focus

When monitoring critical minerals, key OSINT indicators include:

* export restrictions and trade policies
* production concentration by country
* new mining investments
* supply disruptions (conflicts, sanctions)
* price volatility

---

## Strategic Relevance

Critical minerals represent a key leverage point in the global semiconductor supply chain.

Control over these materials can influence:

* technological competitiveness
* industrial resilience
* national security strategies
