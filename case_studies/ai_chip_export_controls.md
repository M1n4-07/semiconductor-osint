# Case Study: AI Chips and Export Controls

## Overview

Advanced AI chips are a critical component of modern computing, enabling artificial intelligence, data centers, and high-performance computing.

These chips have become a central focus of geopolitical competition due to their dual-use nature and strategic importance.

## Data & Evidence

Key indicators illustrate the impact of export controls on AI chips:

* revenue exposure of U.S. firms to the Chinese market
* decline or redirection of high-performance chip exports
* increased investment in domestic semiconductor capabilities in China

For example:

* China has historically represented a significant share of revenue for companies like NVIDIA
* export restrictions have led to the development of downgraded chips for restricted markets
* Chinese firms are increasing investment in domestic AI chip development

These trends can be monitored through company reports, trade data, and policy announcements.
## OSINT Methodology

This case can be monitored using a combination of open-source data:

* trade data → tracking semiconductor exports and flows
* company disclosures → earnings reports and market exposure
* policy documents → export control regulations
* news monitoring → announcements and market reactions

Analytical approach:

1. identify key actors (NVIDIA, AMD, TSMC)
2. track regulatory changes
3. monitor trade and revenue data
4. assess shifts in supply chain behavior

## Early Signals to Watch

Key early indicators of change include:

* new export control updates or licensing requirements
* launch of alternative AI chips for restricted markets
* increased investment in Chinese semiconductor firms
* partnerships aimed at supply chain diversification
* shifts in global semiconductor trade flows
