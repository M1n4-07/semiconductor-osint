# Satellite and GEOINT for Semiconductor OSINT

Satellite imagery (GEOINT) can be used to monitor semiconductor manufacturing facilities, supply chain infrastructure, and industrial expansion.

This provides visual confirmation of developments that may not yet be reflected in official data.

---

## Why GEOINT Matters

Satellite analysis allows:

- tracking of new fab construction
- monitoring expansion of existing facilities
- verification of industrial activity
- detection of supply chain infrastructure

---

## Key Use Cases

### Fab Construction

Monitor:

- new semiconductor fabs
- expansion of existing facilities
- timelines of construction projects

---

### Industrial Infrastructure

Track:

- gas plants
- logistics hubs
- supporting industrial facilities

---

### Geopolitical Monitoring

Observe:

- strategic industrial zones
- government-backed projects
- regional concentration of semiconductor activity

---

**Use:**

- monitoring changes over time

### Planet Labs

Link: https://www.planet.com/

## Targets to Monitor

### Major Foundries

- TSMC fabs (Taiwan, U.S.)
- Samsung fabs (South Korea, U.S.)
- Intel fabs (U.S., Europe)

---

### Emerging Facilities

- new fabs in the United States (CHIPS Act)
- European semiconductor projects
- Chinese semiconductor expansion

---

## OSINT Indicators

- construction progress (groundbreaking, expansion)
- increased activity at industrial sites
- new infrastructure development
- changes in surrounding logistics networks

---

## Analytical Techniques

### Time-Series Analysis

Compare images over time to detect:

- construction progress
- operational changes
- expansion patterns

---

### Cross-Referencing

Combine satellite data with:

- company announcements
- government subsidies
- trade and investment data

---

## Limitations

- resolution constraints
- weather and cloud coverage
- interpretation challenges
- access restrictions for high-resolution data

---

## Strategic Relevance

GEOINT provides a unique layer of intelligence that complements:

- trade data
- policy analysis
- corporate reporting

It allows analysts to validate and anticipate developments in the semiconductor supply chain.
