# Semiconductor Foundries

Foundries are companies that manufacture semiconductor chips designed by fabless firms.

They are a central pillar of the semiconductor supply chain and a key point of geopolitical and economic competition.

---

## Leading Foundries

### TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)

**Location:** Taiwan
**Role:** Global leader in advanced semiconductor manufacturing

**Capabilities:**

* Leading-edge nodes (3nm, 5nm)
* Major supplier for companies like Apple, NVIDIA, AMD

**Strategic Importance:**

* Dominates advanced chip production globally
* Critical to global technology supply chains

**Risks:**

* geopolitical tensions involving Taiwan
* concentration of advanced manufacturing

---

### Samsung Electronics

**Location:** South Korea
**Role:** Integrated device manufacturer and foundry

**Capabilities:**

* advanced nodes (3nm, 5nm)
* strong position in memory + logic chips

**Strategic Importance:**

* key competitor to TSMC
* important for supply diversification

**Risks:**

* high capital intensity
* technological competition pressures

---

### Intel

**Location:** United States
**Role:** Integrated manufacturer expanding into foundry services

**Capabilities:**

* advanced manufacturing (Intel 7, Intel 4)
* expanding foundry business (Intel Foundry Services)

**Strategic Importance:**

* central to U.S. industrial policy (CHIPS Act)
* key player in reshoring semiconductor production

**Risks:**

* delays in technology roadmap
* competition with TSMC and Samsung

---

### SMIC (Semiconductor Manufacturing International Corporation)

**Location:** China
**Role:** Leading Chinese foundry

**Capabilities:**

* mature nodes
* limited access to advanced lithography

**Strategic Importance:**

* central to China’s semiconductor self-sufficiency strategy

**Risks:**

* export controls
* restricted access to advanced equipment

---

### GlobalFoundries

**Location:** United States
**Role:** Specialized foundry (non-leading edge)

**Capabilities:**

* mature nodes
* focus on automotive, IoT, and industrial chips

**Strategic Importance:**

* diversification of supply chains
* resilience for legacy nodes

**Risks:**

* not competitive at cutting-edge nodes

---

## OSINT Indicators to Monitor

Key signals when analyzing foundries:

* fab construction and expansion (new facilities)
* technological node development (e.g., 3nm, 2nm)
* government subsidies and industrial policy
* supply agreements with major tech companies
* export controls affecting equipment access
* geopolitical tensions in key regions

---

## Strategic Relevance

Foundries represent one of the most critical chokepoints in the global semiconductor supply chain.

Control over advanced manufacturing capacity influences:

* technological leadership
* economic competitiveness
* national security
