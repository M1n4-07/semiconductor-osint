# Semiconductor Equipment Companies

Semiconductor manufacturing depends on highly specialized equipment used in lithography, etching, deposition, and inspection.

This segment represents one of the most critical chokepoints in the global semiconductor supply chain.

---

## Key Equipment Segments

* Lithography (patterning circuits on wafers)
* Etching (removing material)
* Deposition (adding material layers)
* Inspection & metrology (quality control)

---

## Leading Companies

### ASML

**Location:** Netherlands
**Focus:** Lithography (EUV and DUV)

**Role:**

* global monopoly in EUV lithography
* essential for advanced chip manufacturing (5nm and below)

**Strategic Importance:**

* critical chokepoint in semiconductor production
* central to export control policies

**Risks:**

* export restrictions to China
* geopolitical pressure from the U.S. and allies

---

### Applied Materials

**Location:** United States
**Focus:** Deposition, etching, materials engineering

**Role:**

* one of the largest semiconductor equipment suppliers
* supports multiple stages of chip production

**Strategic Importance:**

* critical for scaling manufacturing processes
* key supplier to foundries globally

**Risks:**

* exposure to export controls
* dependency on global semiconductor demand

---

### Lam Research

**Location:** United States
**Focus:** Etching and deposition

**Role:**

* major provider of wafer fabrication equipment
* strong presence in advanced manufacturing

**Strategic Importance:**

* essential for chip fabrication processes
* key player in supply chain resilience

**Risks:**

* export restrictions
* concentration of customers in Asia

---

### KLA Corporation

**Location:** United States
**Focus:** Inspection and metrology

**Role:**

* leader in process control and defect inspection
* ensures quality and yield in chip production

**Strategic Importance:**

* critical for maintaining advanced manufacturing standards

**Risks:**

* reliance on advanced fabs
* export control exposure

---

### Tokyo Electron

**Location:** Japan
**Focus:** Etching, deposition, cleaning

**Role:**

* major Japanese supplier of semiconductor equipment
* strong presence in global supply chains

**Strategic Importance:**

* key partner for foundries and IDMs
* important for diversification of supply

**Risks:**

* exposure to export controls
* geopolitical alignment pressures

---

## OSINT Indicators to Monitor

Key signals when analyzing equipment companies:

* export control policies affecting equipment sales
* technological advancements (EUV, High-NA EUV)
* partnerships with foundries
* government pressure or restrictions
* supply chain bottlenecks in machinery
* capital expenditure (capex) trends from chipmakers

---

## Strategic Relevance

Semiconductor equipment represents one of the most powerful control points in the global chip industry.

Control over advanced equipment enables:

* technological leadership
* restriction of competitors' capabilities
* influence over global supply chains

---

## Key Insight

Unlike chips themselves, semiconductor equipment is:

* harder to replicate
* concentrated in a small number of companies
* deeply tied to national security strategies
