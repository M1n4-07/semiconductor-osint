# Trade Data for Semiconductor OSINT

Trade data is one of the most valuable sources for monitoring the semiconductor supply chain.

It allows analysts to track flows of materials, equipment, and components across countries and companies.

---

## Why Trade Data Matters

Trade data enables:

- identification of supply chain dependencies
- tracking of critical material flows
- detection of shifts in global production
- monitoring of export controls impact
- mapping of supplier networks

---

## Key Platforms

### UN Comtrade

Link: https://comtrade.un.org/

**Description:**
Global database of official international trade statistics.

**Use Cases:**
- track exports/imports of semiconductors and materials
- identify major producing and consuming countries
- monitor long-term trends

---

### Trade Map (International Trade Centre)

Link: https://www.trademap.org/

**Description:**
Detailed bilateral trade data and country-level statistics.

**Use Cases:**
- analyze trade relationships between countries
- identify dependencies and concentration risks

---

### Panjiva

Link: https://panjiva.com/

**Description:**
Commercial platform with shipment-level data.

**Use Cases:**
- track specific companies and shipments
- identify suppliers and buyers
- monitor supply chain networks

---

### ImportGenius

Link: https://www.importgenius.com/

**Description:**
Customs and shipping data platform.

**Use Cases:**
- track import/export activity of companies
- monitor supply chain movements in near real-time

---

## Analytical Techniques

### 1. Dependency Mapping

Identify:

- top exporters
- top importers
- concentration ratios

---

### 2. Trend Analysis

Track:

- growth or decline in trade flows
- sudden disruptions
- seasonal or structural changes

---

### 3. Shock Detection

Look for:

- sudden drops in exports
- spikes in imports
- rerouting of supply chains

---

### 4. Cross-Referencing

Combine trade data with:

- export control announcements
- company earnings reports
- geopolitical events

---

## Limitations

Trade data has several constraints:

- reporting delays (often months)
- limited granularity for advanced technologies
- classification issues (HS codes)
- lack of visibility on informal or restricted flows

---

## Strategic Relevance

Trade data provides one of the clearest windows into the real structure of the semiconductor supply chain.

It helps analysts move from:

- theoretical understanding  
to  
- evidence-based intelligence
